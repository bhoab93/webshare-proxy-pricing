## Smart Bandwidth-Saving Tactics That Cut Your Real Cost in Half

Buying more bandwidth is one solution. Burning less is the smarter one. A handful of tactics that compound nicely:

**Strip everything but HTML.** Block images, fonts, and tracking pixels at the request level. Most scraping libraries support resource-type filtering. This single change cuts bandwidth by 40 to 70 percent on image-heavy sites.

**Cache aggressively.** If you're scraping the same product page twice in 48 hours, you're paying twice for the same data. Even a basic Redis layer with a 24-hour TTL slashes wasted requests on long-running monitoring jobs.

**Use sticky sessions where the target permits.** A 10-minute sticky session that successfully scrapes 30 product pages costs less than 30 fresh rotating connections that each carry their own TLS handshake overhead.

**Validate before you scrape at scale.** Run a 100-page test on the cheapest tier first. Measure bytes-per-page. Then scale. The number of teams that skip this step and discover their 1TB plan was actually a 250GB job is genuinely surprising.

**Fix your retry logic.** A porly written retry loop can turn one failed request into ten. Cap retries at three with exponential backoff and you'll often save more bandwidth than upgrading a tier would have given you.
